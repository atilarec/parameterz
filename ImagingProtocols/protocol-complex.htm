The standard presentation of the subcostal coronal (4-chamber) views is "anatomic": index mark on the transducer to patient left, patient left displayed on the right side of monitor.

90 degrees clockwise from coronal (index mark is posterior)

The standard presentation of the parasternal long axis is with the index mark oriented to the base of the heart (regardless of direction) i.e., the base (atria) on the right side of the monitor and the apex on the left side.

90 degrees clockwise from LAX orientation, regardless of the position of the heart within the chest.

90 CW rotated from LSB: LAX ("ductus cut") shows left pulmonary veins nicely

90 CW rotated from RSB: LAX (SVC) shows right pulmonary veins nicely

The standard presentation of the apical view is "anatomic": index mark on the transducer to patient left, patient left displayed on the right side of monitor, regardless of the position of the heart within the chest.

The standard presentation of the arch is with the ascending aorta to the left of the screen (anterior) and the descending aorta to the right of the screen (posterior) i.e., the orientation marker to the left clavicle for left arch; to the right clavicle for right arch.
