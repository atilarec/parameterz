Approximately 30-45 degrees CCW rotated from coronal

90 CW rotated from LSB: LAX ("ductus cut") shows left pulmonary veins nicely

90 CW rotated from RSB: LAX (SVC) shows right pulmonary veins nicely

Oriented like an apical 4 chamber, but positioned medially (low parasternal window)

The standard presentation of the arch is with the ascending aorta to the left of the screen (anterior) and the descending aorta to the right of the screen (posterior) i.e., the orientation marker to the left clavicle for left arch; to the right clavicle for right arch.
