Discriminant Score:

Aortic root dimension is taken as the maximum dimension at the level of the sinuses of Valsalva, obtained from the parasternal long-axis view.

The long-axis length of the left ventricle is measured as the distance from the plane of the mitral valve annulus to the apex of the ventricle.

The long axis of the heart is measured from the apical four chamber view as the distance from the crux of the heart to the apical endocardium (either right or left ventricle, whichever formed the apex of the heart).

The antero-posterior early diastolic dimension of the mitral annulus is taken from the long-axis parasternal image.

The lateral early diastolic dimension of the mitral annulus is taken from the apical four-chamber image.
