Goal

To work through an aortic root z-score calculation according to Gautier et al., using example data.

Calculate the Z-Score

The z-scores are calculated according to the formula supplied in the text:

            
    Z = [ln(measured diameter) - ln(predicted mean diameter)]/(SD),
    Z = [ln(measured diameter) - (a.ln(x) + b)]/(SD)

Interestingly, when they get around to presenting the actual data (Table 2), they use a different version of the formula- the data for the aortic annulus for a male subject is reported as:

            
    Z = [ln(d) - 3.1–0.49 * ln(BSA)]/0.1

This example calculation assumes a male patient with a BSA of 1.45 m2 and a measured aortic annulus of 30.0 mm:

            
    Z = [ln(30) - 3.1-0.49 * ln(1.45)]/0.1    
    
    which resolves to
    Z = [3.4012 - 3.1-0.49 * 0.3716]/0.1
    
    ...
    Z = [3.4012 - 3.1-0.1821]/0.1  
    
    ...
    Z = [0.1191]/0.1
    
    which resolves to
    Z = 1.191

(The example worked in the article is rounded to "1.2")

Calculate the Upper Limit of Normal

According to the article, the formula for prediction of the upper limit of normal (ULN) has the form:


    e(b + 2SD)*xa
    
    or, for the sinuses of Valsalva
    
    y = 26.95 * BSA0.49

Working through this calculation with the same assumptions (bsa = 1.45 m2, male):

        
    y = 26.95 * (BSA)0.49
    
    which resolves to
    y = 26.95 * 1.450.49
    
    ...
    y = 26.95 * 1.1997
    
    which resolves to
    y = 32.33
